What is Digital Forensics ?

Digital forensics is the comprehensive practice of handling digital data for the purpose of legal investigation. It encompasses the specialized techniques required to identify, secure, examine, and report on electronic evidence. Beyond simply recovering data, the discipline mandates that all evidence be handled in a way that preserves its forensic integrity. This ensures that the final results are legally defensible, effectively bridging the gap between raw binary data and courtroom-ready evidence.

Tools used in Digital Forensics :

Autopsy : a digital forensics platform and graphical interface to "The Sleuth Kit" and other digital forensics tools. It is used by law enforcement, military, and corporate examiners to investigate the file systems of computers and recover deleted data. (https://www.autopsy.com/).

Eric Zimmerman's tools : Eric Zimmerman is a former FBI agent and a SANS instructor who has developed a suite of open-source digital forensics tools that are now considered industry standards. (https://ericzimmerman.github.io/#!index.md)
